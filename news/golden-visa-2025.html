Golden Visa 2025: Changes across Europe

Overview

The Golden Visa programs, which have been a cornerstone of residency-by-investment schemes in Europe, are at a pivotal juncture. From Hungary’s surprising reintroduction to Spain’s potential reforms, these programs are evolving in response to economic demands and public scrutiny. As one expert from The Financial Times notes, "These shifts indicate a new era of balancing foreign investments with local priorities."

Hungary’s Reintroduction

Hungary has reignited interest with its reintroduction of the Golden Visa program. According to Reuters, the program focuses on high-compliance standards and investments in sectors critical to Hungary’s growth. The country aims to position itself as a viable gateway to Central Europe for global investors.

Spain’s Golden Visa Reforms

In contrast, Spain is contemplating a dramatic shift. Plans to end real estate-based Golden Visas, first announced in April 2024, signal the nation’s intent to prioritize housing affordability over speculative investments. "This marks a significant turn in policy," reported BBC News, adding that Spain’s reforms may influence other EU nations.

Other EU Countries Retaining Golden Visas

Elsewhere, the landscape remains diverse. Countries like Italy, Greece, and Malta continue to offer Golden Visas with adjusted frameworks to meet both local and EU standards:

  • Italy - Encourages sustainable growth through investments in businesses, bonds, and philanthropy (Forbes).
  • Greece - Maintains its allure with low entry thresholds but has increased real estate investment requirements in major areas (Euronews).
  • Malta - Tightens due diligence to align with EU anti-money laundering directives (EU Observer).

The broad spectrum of policies reflects the need for investors to be both informed and strategic.
